Module Focus:
Information Seeking and Providing, Clarifying & Confirming Information

Aims:

  • Ask for essential information clearly (locations, times, rates, services)
  • Provide accurate, concise information to guests (dates, times, prices, service options)
  • Confirm understanding and next steps
  • Enhance clarity and politeness in information exchanges

Language Skills Focus:

Listening: dialogues discussing key details from inquiries and notices.

Speaking: ask precise questions; give clear, structured responses.

Reading: two texts interpreting schedules, price lists, service descriptors with MC or True/False questions.

Writing: compose clear information emails/messages with actionable next steps.


Language Revision: Present Simple Expanded - Present Continuous - Future Forms (basic introductions).


Language Functions: Seeking & Providing Information, clarifying to avoid misunderstanding, confirming information, documenting & recording details.

III. Language Revision
Present Simple Expanded
Regular and some irregular verbs in third person singular.
Adverbs of frequency: always, usually, often, sometimes, never
Present Continuous
Form: am/are/is + verb-ing
Use: actions happening now, temporary actions, future arrangements: "I am meeting him tomorrow."
Future Forms (basic introductions)
Be going to: intention, "I am going to study abroad."
Present Continuous for future arrangements: "I am meeting him tomorrow."
Simple future with will/shall (very basic): "OK. I will help you."
(Not planned)
LANGUAGE FUNCTIONS
Seeking information: "Do you have a map of the area?"
Providing information: "Ferry tickets are 8,00 euros."
Clarifying: "This is the front desk. You have asked for a taxi tomorrow at 7:00AM. Correct?"
Confirming: "We have received your e-mail, and we confirm your reservation."
Documenting: "I will email or text you a summary of these details."
