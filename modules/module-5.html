Second Hour

Module Focus:
Handling Complaints and Difficult Customers

Aims:

  • Complaint handling,
  • Manage dissatisfaction calmly,
  • Maintain empathetic, professional tone.

Language Skills Focus:

Listening: identify main issue, emotions, and needs

Speaking: empathise, restate, respond politely to a complaint

Reading: professional complaint responses, apologising in writing

Writing: stating complaints, reporting complaints


Language Revision:

I would like... / I want to...

Would you...? Could I...?


Language Functions:

Making a complaint, Identifying problems, Expressing feelings, Suggesting and finding solutions, Handling complaints. Stating policies / Apologising

I. Useful Language

INSTRUCTIONS:
Read the expressions below. Then, listen to them and practise saying them after the Robot.
Robot
Section 1: Expressions used when making a complaint:
1. "I’m afraid there’s a problem with the key card."
2. "I’m not happy with the service in the restaurant."
3. "Could you do something about this?"

Section 2: Expressions used to respond to a complaint (to restate, empathise, apologise):
1. "I’m sorry about your accident."
2. "I understand that you would like to change rooms."
3. "My apologies for the inconvenience."

Section 3: Expressions used for proposing solutions:
1. "Let me take care of this for you right away."
2. "I can order a taxi for you."
3. "Let me take care of this for you right away."
