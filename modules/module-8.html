This is a 3-part activity to revise a number of phrasal verbs with get and take used in common expressions in conversational English.
Part A — INSTRUCTIONS:
To begin, read this table. Note down any expressions you are not familiar with. Take notes. Please, use your notebook.
Get Expressions
Meanings of “get” Obtain (buy), receive, become (change of state), and arrive (reach a place).
Examples of use get married, get ready, get lost, get home, get in touch.
Phrasal Verbs get up, get on/off (transport), get on with (someone), get over (a sickness), get rid of.
Take Expressions
Meanings of “take” “take” is used in fixed phrases and as part of daily routines.
Everyday actions take a photo, take a break/rest, take a shower/bath, take a seat.
Other expressions take care of, take part in, take place, take a risk, take time.
Phrasal Verbs take off (for planes or clothes), and take up (a new hobby).
