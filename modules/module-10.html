Purpose 1: Seeking Information

Standard phrases used in tourism contexts, organized by purpose. They include seeking information, providing information, clarifying to avoid misunderstandings, confirming information, and documenting/recording details. Each section includes examples you can adapt to your language and situation.

1.
Seeking Information (asking for information)

General inquiries

Could you tell me where the [location] is?
Do you have a map of the area?
Where can I find public transport to [destination]?
What are the must-see attractions in [city/area]?

Hours, tickets, and access

What are the opening hours for [museum/attraction]?
How much is an adult/child ticket?
Are there any discounts or family passes available?
Is advance booking required?

Directions and transport

Which is the best way to get to [place] from here?
Do you have a timetable for buses/trains to [destination]?
Is there a taxi stand or ride-sharing option nearby?
Are there accessible routes or facilities?

Services and facilities

Do you offer free Wi-Fi, and where is the signal strongest?
Is there a luggage storage/locker facility available?
Are there guided tours in [language]?
Is there a restaurant/cafe on site, and what are its hours?

Safety and etiquette

Are there any safety advisories I should know about?
Are there dress code requirements or cultural etiquette to observe?
Is tipping customary here, and if so, how much?

Miscellaneous

Where can I exchange currency?
Do you accept credit cards or is cash required?
Is tipping included in the bill?

Purpose 2: Providing Information

2.
Providing Information (giving information clearly)

General directions

To get to [place], head south on [street], turn left at [landmark], it’s about a 10-minute walk.
The entrance to [attraction] is on [street], next to [landmark].

Hours and pricing

[Attraction] opens at [time] and closes at [time]. Last entry is at [time].
Adult tickets cost [amount], children/seniors/student tickets cost [amount]. Family passes are [amount].

Services and facilities

We offer free Wi-Fi in the lobby; password is [password].
Luggage storage is available from [time] to [time] near the main entrance.
Guided tours are available in [languages] at [times]; advance booking is recommended.

Accessibility and accommodations

There are wheelchair-accessible entrances on [location], with elevators on [level/floor].
Restrooms are located on [floor/area].

Safety and rules

Photography is allowed in most areas, except where signs indicate otherwise.
No flash photography in the exhibit halls.
Smoking is prohibited inside the building.

Purpose 3: Clarifying

3.
Clarifying to Avoid Misunderstanding (clarifying and confirming)

Clarification requests

Just to confirm, did you say [paraphrase of what you heard]?
Are you saying the tour starts at [time] and ends at [time]?
So the price is [amount] for adults and [amount] for children, correct?

Repeating for accuracy

Let me repeat the details to make sure I’ve got them right: [summary].
Could you please confirm the meeting point is [location] at [time]?

Handling ambiguity

If that line refers to [item A] or [item B], which one did you mean?
I want to ensure I understand: is the refundable option valid until [date]?

Language and comprehension

Would you like me to explain that in simpler terms or in another language?
Is there a particular term you’d like me to use or avoid?

Purpose 4: Confirming

4.
Confirming Information (confirmations and approvals)

Booking confirmations

Your booking is confirmed for [date] at [time] for [number] people. Confirmation number: [number].
I have reserved your tickets for [attraction] in the language [language]. Please present this code: [code].

Payment confirmations

Payment has been received. Your receipt number is [receipt].
Would you like me to email a receipt to [email]?

Schedule confirmations

The tour will depart from [location] at [time] sharp. Please arrive 10 minutes early.
The flight/train/bus is scheduled to depart at [time] and arrive at [time].

Special requests

I’ve noted your accessibility requirements and dietary restrictions. We’ll do our best to accommodate them.
Your pickup/transfer in [city] is confirmed for [time] with [service/provider].

Purpose 5: Documenting

5.
Documenting and Recording Details (note-taking and records)

Collecting essential information

Full name: ____________
Contact number: ____________
Email address: ____________
Number of travelers (adults/children): ____________
Booking reference: ____________
Date, time, and location of activity/meeting: ____________
Special requirements (dietary, accessibility, language): ____________

Verbal handoffs and receipts

I will email or text you a summary of these details. Do you prefer email or SMS?
Here is an itemized receipt/itinerary. Please review and let me know if anything is incorrect.

Incident and feedback logging

If you need to report an issue, please describe what happened, where, when, and who was involved.
We appreciate your feedback. Please rate your experience from 1 to 5 and add any comments.

Data privacy reminder

Your information will be used to process your booking and for related communications. It will not be shared without your consent, except as required by law.
