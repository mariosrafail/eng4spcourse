IV. Language Revision
INSTRUCTIONS:
Study the language functions and the way to combine two sentences to confirm your understanding of guest's comments.
LANGUAGE FUNCTIONS
Identifying problems and stating policies empathising and resolving problems (using SUBORDINATE NOUN CLAUSES).
COMMON VERBS used with NOUN CLAUSES:
Thinking verbs: know, think, believe, hope, understand, realize.
Perception Verbs (Sensing): see, hear, feel, notice.
Discovery/Certainty Verbs: find out, prove, confirm.
Putting two thoughts together to make one sentence:
SENTENCE 1: You know 'something'. [What?]
SENTENCE 2: Our hotel's policy is 'No pets allowed'.
TOGETHER: You know that our hotel's policy is 'No pets allowed'.

V. Reading

READING TEXT
INSTRUCTIONS: Read the text below.

The HEARD method is used by tourist professionals to manage complaints and turn negative guest experiences into positive interactions. The acronym stands for Hear, Empathise, Apologise, Resolve, and Diagnose.

  1. Hear – Listen actively to the guest expressing their complaints, without interrupting
  2. Empathise – Respond with phrases, "I can see why that would be disappointing."
  3. Apologise – offer an apology for the inconvenience
  4. Resolve – suggest a solution, a room change? A future discount?
  5. Diagnose – find out why this problem happened for a better future.
